Wildlife Tourism

What is wildlife tourism?

Wildlife tourism is an aspect of tourism in many countries that focuses on observing and interacting with native animals and plants in their natural environment.
At it's most basic, wildlife tourism involves engaging with wildlife in their natural habitats, either actively (such as hunting or gathering) or passively (such as observation and photography).
Wildlife tourism includes safari hunting and other high-intervention activities, and eco- and animal-friendly tours.

What are the countries that wildlife tourism are importatnt to them ?

Numerous nations in Africa and South America, as well as Australia, India, Canada, Indonesia, Bangladesh, Malaysia, Sri Lanka, and the Maldives, among others, have significant wildlife tourism sectors.
It has grown dramatically and quickly in recent years across the globe, and many aspects of it are closely related to eco-tourism and sustainable tourism.

Wildlife tourism styles(types).

There are some types of wildlife tourism such as,

  1. Birds Wathching
  2. Whale Watching
  3. Leopard Watching
  4. Primates Watching
  5. Elephant Safari

Advantages of wildlife tourism.

1.Encounters with wildlife that are breathtaking.

The opportunity to experience breath-taking wildlife encounters is a major attraction of wildlife tourism.
Seeing majestic animals in their natural habitats, such as elephants, lions or whales, can instill a deep appreciation for the greatness of nature.

2.Educational Opportunities.

Wildlife tourism is a wonderful way to educate people and learn about different species and ecosystems.
Visitors are frequently accompanied by tour guides and experts who provide insightful information about the creatures, their behavior and the delicate habitats in which they live.

Disadvantages of wildlife tourism.

1.Spreding diseases.

Unintentionally, wildlife tourism can help diseases spread from animals to people. Visitors as well as animal populations may be at risk if tourists and wildlife come into close contact and help spread diseases.

2.Exploitation Risk.

Unreliable operators can exploit wildlife tourism for financial gain by using unethical methods such as baiting animals or overstocking habitats to attract more visitors.
Such exploitation can have negative effects on animals and their environment.
